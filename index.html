Profile

I study how social structures shape inequality, mobility, and institutional change across time and place. My work integrates sociological theory, quantitative modeling, computational analysis, and qualitative inquiry to examine how evidence becomes explanation.

My research is grounded in a simple principle: good social science should make complex social realities more visible, more explainable, and more accountable.

Research design is not only a technical procedure. It is the architecture through which social reality becomes observable, interpretable, and open to explanation.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching emphasizes methodological judgment, critical thinking, and disciplined use of evidence. In the age of artificial intelligence, students need more than answers. They need defensible reasoning, interpretive discipline, and the ability to explain why evidence matters.
