The Architecture of Social Inquiry

I study how inequality is produced, measured, and explained across societies. My work connects sociological theory with comparative research, quantitative modeling, qualitative inquiry, and computational methods.

I focus on how research design turns social complexity into evidence, explanation, and public relevance.

Research design is the structure that makes social reality visible, evidence credible, and explanation possible.
