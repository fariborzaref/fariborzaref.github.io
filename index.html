The Architecture of Social Inquiry

I study how inequality is produced, measured, interpreted, and explained across societies. My work brings together sociological theory, comparative research, computational analysis, quantitative modeling, and qualitative inquiry.

Across my research, teaching, and open repositories, I focus on one central problem: how social science can move from complex social reality to defensible evidence, explanation, and public relevance.

My work treats research design not as a technical formality, but as the structure through which social reality becomes visible, evidence becomes credible, and explanation becomes possible.
